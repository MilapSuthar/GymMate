# GymMate — The Investable Version

*If I were writing the cheque, this is the GymMate I'd be backing.*

The thesis: **a habit-forming social fitness app where the moat is the schedule + gym graph nobody else has.** Strava owns your running. Hinge owns your dating. GymMate owns the question "who am I lifting with this week, and where?"

To get there, you need three engines firing in parallel:

1. **The dopamine engine** — the reason users open the app today.
2. **The lock-in engine** — the reason users can't leave next year.
3. **The growth engine** — the reason new users keep arriving for free.

Everything below maps to one of those three.

---

## 1. THE DOPAMINE ENGINE — daily reasons to open the app

### Variable reward — the swipe deck, but smarter

- **Daily fresh batch of 10.** When you open the app, you get 10 new profiles. After that, "Come back tomorrow for more." This is Hinge's exact pattern. It creates a *window* — a reason to open the app every morning, every evening. Without a daily cap, the deck feels infinite (no scarcity) AND empty fast (you run out in a week). The cap fixes both.
- **Super-Lifts** (your version of Tinder's Super-Like). Free users get 1 per day. Plus users get 5. They cost nothing to send, but the receiver sees a gold border around your card, so they're scarce *socially*. This is pure dopamine — sending one feels like a real move, receiving one feels like being chosen.
- **Boost** — £1.99 for "I'm top of everyone's deck in my gym for 30 minutes." The price is the dopamine; you're literally buying anticipation.
- **Quick reactions on photos.** Hinge's killer mechanic: instead of liking the *whole profile*, you tap a heart on one specific photo or prompt and add a note. This makes "I matched" feel less like luck and more like someone reacted to *you*. It also makes the chat opener obvious: "Loved that you bench at 5 AM — what gym?"

### Notifications calibrated like a slot machine

Not "you have a new match" — too generic. Instead:

- "Sarah and 3 others liked your card this morning" → loss aversion
- "Two people at your gym are looking for a partner now" → urgency
- "Your matches expire in 24h — say hi?" (Bumble's mechanic, but applied to all matches, not gender-gated)
- "Your bench-PR overlap with Alex is 5kg — perfect spotter match" → curiosity
- "Your streak is at 11 days. Don't break it." → Duolingo

Every notification has a *specific person, a specific reason, a specific time bound*. Generic = ignored.

### The status game

- **Streaks** on three axes: workout-logging streak, app-open streak, helpful-answer streak (Help Board). Visible. Public. Painful to break.
- **Reputation** on the Help Board: upvotes give you GymMate Karma. At 1,000 karma you get a Verified Lifter badge. At 10,000 you get into the trainer-applicant fast-track. Status to lose = retention.
- **Achievements**: First Match. 100 Workouts Logged. 10 Helpful Answers. Travel Buddy (matched in 3 cities). Crew Captain (started a 5-person squad). Display them on your profile.
- **The leaderboard you don't ask for**: weekly volume lifted, weekly distance run, weekly nutrition consistency, ranked among your friends only. Strava nailed this.

### "Who's at the gym right now"

Tap into your gym → see anonymized icons of people there. Tap one → "👋 Say hi at the squat rack?" — sends a one-tap intro that disappears if not replied to in 30 mins. This is the **single feature that would make me check the app every time I walk into a gym**, and nobody has it. It's the killer use case for users in their *home* gym.

## 2. THE LOCK-IN ENGINE — the moat that keeps users a year later

### The workout journal becomes an emotional asset

Every set you log, every PR you hit, every progress photo you upload is value the user can't recreate elsewhere. The longer they use GymMate, the more painful it is to leave.

- **Visual progress timeline** — your transformation in a swipeable photo strip. "On this day a year ago you couldn't squat 60kg. Now you squat 100kg." Push this as a notification on the anniversary. Make it shareable to Instagram.
- **Year in Lifting** — December's "Wrapped." Total volume lifted (in tonnes), days trained, PRs, calories tracked, partners met. A shareable card. Spotify-Wrapped levels of free distribution.
- **Strength-vs-time charts** for every lift. Annotated with PRs, deload weeks, injuries. Users will keep paying just to see this graph.

### The social graph

Right now you have matches. That's a weak tie. Build three more relationship tiers:

- **Crew** (3-10 people) — a permanent group chat + shared workout calendar. "Tuesday 6 PM at the gym?" "+1." Pure utility. This is what WhatsApp groups are doing today; GymMate should own it with the workout layer integrated.
- **Spotters** — people you've actually trained with. Auto-suggested after a confirmed session. Becomes a 1-tap "you again?" reconnect.
- **Following** — like Instagram. One-way, public. Your favourite trainer, your inspo lifter. Their PRs show up in your feed.

The match is the *first* relationship in a stack of four. The other three are why people stay.

### The Reddit layer — the Help Board as a real community

The Help Board today is a flat Q&A. To be Reddit-grade:

- **Topic communities**: `/squats`, `/cutting`, `/female-lifters`, `/over-40`, `/calisthenics`, `/powerlifting`, `/marathon`, `/injury-recovery`. Each has its own feed, own mods, own culture.
- **Upvote / downvote** with karma totals visible on the profile.
- **Best of the Week** — pushed every Sunday evening as a notification. "5 most-loved tips this week" → a re-engagement event.
- **AMAs** with verified pros. "Today at 8 PM: a Team GB powerlifter is taking questions." Live engagement spike.
- **Polls** — "What's your favourite squat depth?" Quick, lazy, addictive. Reddit-grade engagement on autopilot.

Done right, the Help Board becomes the reason people open the app on rest days. That doubles your retention surface.

## 3. THE GROWTH ENGINE — viral loops + travel as a wedge

### Travel mode (this is your most underrated feature)

When a user travels to a new city — for work, a competition, a holiday — they currently have no way to keep training socially. They go to a hotel gym alone. GymMate solves it:

- **Visiting mode**: "I'm in Barcelona June 12-18." Locals see you as a "Visiting Lifter" badge on the deck. You see locals first.
- **Travel hubs**: curated 5-best-gyms guides in 50 cities, contributed by locals (they get karma for it).
- **Day-pass integrations**: PureGym, Anytime Fitness, ClassPass — book a session straight from the app.
- **"Lifter in town" notification** to locals in that city. "5 visitors are looking for partners this week."

This converts a *real recurring need* (travelling + working out is genuinely painful) into a reason to use the app **even when you've fully saturated your home gym match pool**. It's also a wedge no competitor — including the dating apps — covers.

### Squad challenges (network effects)

5 people form a Crew. They can opt into the weekly Squad Challenge: most volume lifted, most workouts logged, longest streak. The winning Crew gets a free month of Plus + a badge. Now four of your friends are guilting you into not skipping leg day. This is the closest thing fitness has to a Snap Streak — once it's running, it's self-sustaining.

### Referrals that aren't insulting

"Invite 3 lifting friends → 1 month of Plus free for both." Standard. But pair it with: "Invite a buddy who joins your gym → both get Plus for a month." Local referrals are 10x more valuable than random ones because they directly enrich the local match pool. Reward what you actually want.

### Wrapped → Twitter/Instagram

December: "Your GymMate 2026." Shareable. Stats. Best lifts. Best matches. Photos. Hashtag prompts. This is **free user-acquisition theatre** every December, forever.

## 4. THE WEEKLY HABIT CALENDAR

Pick a theme for every day. Push exactly one notification per day, themed:

- **Monday — Match Monday.** Refreshed deck, weekly Super-Lifts reset.
- **Tuesday — Tip Tuesday.** Top Help Board post pushed to your feed.
- **Wednesday — Workout Wednesday.** "Log your week's plan with your Crew."
- **Thursday — Throwback Thursday.** "On this day last year you hit X."
- **Friday — Find a Friend Friday.** Match visibility doubled for 24h.
- **Saturday — Squad Saturday.** Group workout reminders.
- **Sunday — Stats Sunday.** Your weekly recap. Calories, volume, streaks.

Each one is *boring on its own*. Together they make the app feel alive every single day. Snapchat, BeReal, Duolingo all do versions of this. It's free retention.

## 6. MONETIZATION — the stack

### Tiered subscription, optimised for long retention

**Free** (the funnel)
- 10 swipes per day
- 1 Super-Lift per day
- Basic filters (distance only)
- Match → message; max 3 active chats
- Help Board read + write

**Plus — £7.99/mo or £59/yr (35% discount)**
- Unlimited swipes
- 5 Super-Lifts per day
- **Schedule overlap filter** ← *the killer Plus feature*
- See who liked you
- Advanced filters (gender, age, experience, strength range)
- Unlimited rewind
- Read receipts in chat
- Unlimited active chats
- 1 free Boost per month
- Travel mode
- No ads

**Pro — £14.99/mo or £119/yr**
- Everything in Plus
- 3 AI form-checks per month
- 15% off all trainer bookings
- Priority customer support
- Custom workout templates with progressions
- Unlimited Visiting mode (locals see you in any city)
- Profile boost when entering a new gym

### One-off purchases (the impulse layer)
- **Boost** — £1.99 (30 min top-of-deck)
- **Super-Lifts pack** — 5 for £3.99
- **Form Check** — £4.99 single (free tier route into the Pro feature)
- **Wrapped Frame** — premium download with custom photo for sharing, £0.99

### Marketplace (the revenue floor)
- Trainer take rate — 18% application fee
- Meal plan take rate — 22%
- Trainer payout — Stripe Connect Express
- Refund protection — 1.5% margin on every booking → reserve fund

### Brand layer (don't rush, don't pollute)
- Sponsored Gyms — gym chains pay £300/mo to be the "Featured Gym" in a city when users without a gym set tap their location
- Sponsored Crews — a brand can sponsor a 5-person Crew Challenge with prize money
- Curated Marketplace — protein/supplement brands with verified reviews (revenue cut on sales)

**No banner ads. No video ads. Not now, not ever.** The moment users see them, the app becomes Facebook. You're building a product worth paying for; let the paying part of the funnel do the work.
